The Ultimate Office

Solidworks Corporation

2011

01

01 Background

SolidWorks Corporation is a company belonging to Daussault Systemes which produces 3D-modeling software for engineers, called computer-aided design (CAD) software. Having self-taught myself the software in high school, I knew I had to intern with the company when I found out their then-headquarters was ten minutes from my house.

I worked with a marketing team on a project called Let's Go Design. We were given a sizeable amount of money by the company to make the coolest products we could think of and make a web video series to promote SolidWorks to young engineers. I worked with Jeremy Luchini, the lead and face of Let's Go Design, to design and build what we called The Ultimate Office.

02

02 Design

We began by tossing out the conventional four-walls design typical of cubicles and designed a hexagonal structure with a chair that hung from a motorized gantry above. I modeled the PCs, monitors, and drawing board for the model so we could plan out how everything would fit.

We modeled the entire design in SolidWorks first.

03

03 Development

Once we finalized the model, we began construction of the actual Ultimate Office. I helped build a working indoor waterfall from scratch, since we couldn't find a commercial one of the right size, shape, and color. We laid the inside with silicone to waterproof the structure and spray-painted it black.

Building the indoor waterfall.

Building the indoor waterfall.

Finally, we completed parts of the Ultimate Office and then combined them together. The film crew made a spectacular revealing of the final design and we had a lot of fun. Our Ultimate Office was showcased later that year at SolidWorks World to thousands of people. I returned the following summer and helped out when I could with building The Ultimate Baby Stroller as well because I loved the work and the team so much.
